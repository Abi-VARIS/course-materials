# Basecamp Coffee Personality Types

> **Source of truth** for the Coffee Personality Quiz. Use this document for quiz implementation, barista training, and stakeholder review.

---

## The Two Axes

Every coffee drinker lands somewhere on two spectrums:

- **Adventurous ←→ Consistent** — how you approach coffee choices
- **Social ←→ Reflective** — how you experience coffee

These intersect to create four distinct personality types:

|  | Reflective | Social |
|--|-----------|--------|
| **Adventurous** | Bold Adventurer | Flavor Explorer |
| **Consistent** | Cozy Ritualist | Campfire Connector |

Each type maps to a primary customer segment:

| Type | Segment |
|------|---------|
| Bold Adventurer | Identity Seekers |
| Campfire Connector | Connection Cravers |
| Cozy Ritualist | Routine Seekers |
| Flavor Explorer | Mix of all three |

---

## 1. Bold Adventurer

**Tagline:** *"Life's too short for the same cup twice."*

### Description

You treat every cup like a tiny expedition. While other people are reaching for their usual, you're scanning the menu for something you've never tried — a new origin, a wild processing method, an espresso tonic that sounds like it shouldn't work but absolutely does. Coffee isn't just a drink for you; it's a way to learn something new about the world and about yourself. You don't just *like* coffee. You *study* it, one adventurous sip at a time.

### Core Traits

- **Curiosity-driven** — You want to know where the beans came from, how they were processed, and why the barista chose that brew method.
- **Fearlessly experimental** — "Weird" is a compliment. You'll try anything once, and you'll try the strange stuff twice.
- **Quietly passionate** — You don't need to broadcast your coffee knowledge, but when someone asks, you light up.
- **Detail-oriented palate** — You actually taste the "notes of blackberry and cedar" on the bag, and you love that about yourself.

### Coffee Preferences

- **Roast:** Light to medium — you want to taste the origin, not the roaster.
- **Methods:** Pour-over, AeroPress, siphon — anything that lets the bean speak.
- **Flavor profiles:** Complex, bright, unexpected. Ethiopian naturals, Kenyan AA, experimental fermentations.
- **Milk/sugar:** Rarely. You want the full, unfiltered experience.

### Signature Drink Recommendations

1. **Single-Origin Pour-Over** — Ask the barista what's new this week. That's your order.
2. **Espresso Flight** — Three origins, three tiny cups, one big adventure.
3. **Nitro Cold Brew** — Creamy without the cream. Smooth enough to let the bean's character shine.

### Discovery Nudge

> **Try next:** A lavender oat milk latte. Yes, really. Sometimes the best discoveries happen when you let someone else pick the adventure for you.

### Barista Talking Point

*When you spot a Bold Adventurer:* Share the story behind today's single-origin — where it's from, what makes it unusual, how you'd describe the flavor. They want the details. Ask them: "Have you tried our newest origin yet?" They almost certainly want to.

### Share Text

> I'm a **Bold Adventurer**! ☕🏔️ Life's too short for the same cup twice. I'm the one asking the barista what's new, chasing single-origins, and turning every coffee run into a tiny expedition. What's YOUR coffee personality? #BasecampCoffee

## 2. Campfire Connector

**Tagline:** *"The best conversations start with 'Want to grab coffee?'"*

### Description

For you, coffee has never really been about the coffee. It's about the person sitting across from you. You're the one who texts "coffee this week?" to check in on a friend, who knows everyone's order by heart, and who turns a quick latte run into an hour-long catch-up. Your favorite mug is whichever one is in your hand when you're with your people. Basecamp isn't just your coffee shop — it's your living room.

### Core Traits

- **Relationship-first** — You remember that your coworker switched to decaf and you ask how they're doing, not just what they're drinking.
- **Generous by default** — You've bought more "surprise" coffees for friends than you can count.
- **Comfortably loyal** — You have your go-to order, and you love it. Why fix what isn't broken?
- **Warm and inclusive** — If someone's sitting alone at the shop, you're the one who might strike up a conversation.

### Coffee Preferences

- **Roast:** Medium — crowd-pleasing, approachable, easy to love.
- **Methods:** Drip, latte, anything you can hold while talking with your hands.
- **Flavor profiles:** Smooth, sweet, familiar. Chocolate, caramel, nutty warmth.
- **Milk/sugar:** Often. You like your coffee the way you like your friendships — warm and a little sweet.

### Signature Drink Recommendations

1. **Classic Vanilla Latte** — Warm, sweet, reliable. Just like you.
2. **Caramel Macchiato** — A little indulgent, a little special — perfect for sharing a table with someone you love.
3. **Hot Chocolate with a Shot of Espresso** — Because sometimes the best drink is the one that makes your friend say, "Wait, what's that? Can I try it?"

### Discovery Nudge

> **Try next:** A cortado. It's small, it's simple, and it'll give you something new to talk about. Bonus: order two and hand one to a stranger.

### Barista Talking Point

*When you spot a Campfire Connector:* Greet them by name if you can. Ask about the friend they were with last time. If they're ordering for a group, help them make it easy — "Want me to do a tray for you?" They come back for the people as much as the product.

### Share Text

> I'm a **Campfire Connector**! ☕🔥 The best part of coffee is who you share it with. I'm the friend who always texts "coffee this week?" and turns every latte into a two-hour catch-up. What's YOUR coffee personality? #BasecampCoffee

## 3. Cozy Ritualist

**Tagline:** *"Same time, same mug, same magic."*

### Description

Your coffee ritual is sacred, and you wouldn't have it any other way. The quiet morning pour, the specific mug, the exact ratio of cream — these aren't habits, they're anchors. While the world spins faster every day, your coffee is the one thing that's perfectly, reliably yours. You don't need novelty. You need that first sip to taste exactly like it did yesterday, because that's how you know today is going to be okay.

### Core Traits

- **Intentional and present** — You don't scroll while you sip. (Okay, maybe a little. But the first few sips? Those are yours.)
- **Creature of comfort** — You have a favorite seat at Basecamp. The baristas know.
- **Quietly confident** — You don't need to justify your usual. It's yours. It's perfect.
- **Grounded and steady** — People come to you when things feel chaotic, probably because you always seem like you have it together. (It's the coffee ritual. That's the secret.)

### Coffee Preferences

- **Roast:** Medium to dark — rich, full-bodied, deeply satisfying.
- **Methods:** Drip coffee, French press, or a perfectly pulled espresso. Simplicity done well.
- **Flavor profiles:** Warm, rounded, dependable. Dark chocolate, toasted walnut, brown sugar.
- **Milk/sugar:** Consistent — whatever your ratio is, it hasn't changed in years, and it won't.

### Signature Drink Recommendations

1. **House Drip Coffee** — The foundation. The classic. Ask for it in a ceramic mug if you're staying.
2. **Double Espresso** — No frills, no foam. Just coffee doing exactly what coffee does best.
3. **French Press for One** — When you want to slow everything down and make the ritual the whole point.

### Discovery Nudge

> **Try next:** An affogato. It's espresso poured over vanilla ice cream — still simple, still warm-meets-cozy, but with a tiny twist that might just earn a spot in your rotation.

### Barista Talking Point

*When you spot a Cozy Ritualist:* Start making their order when you see them walk in — they'll love that you remembered. Don't push new items hard; instead, mention it casually: "We've got a new dark roast this week if you're ever curious — it's got that same chocolate thing you like." Let them come to it on their own terms.

### Share Text

> I'm a **Cozy Ritualist**! ☕✨ Same time, same mug, same magic. My coffee routine isn't a habit — it's an anchor. The baristas know my order and I wouldn't change a thing. What's YOUR coffee personality? #BasecampCoffee

## 4. Flavor Explorer

**Tagline:** *"I'll have one of everything — and I'm bringing friends."*

### Description

You are the person who makes every coffee run an event. You try the seasonal special on day one. You're the reason the barista made that off-menu creation — because you asked "what would you make if you could make anything?" and then actually ordered it. But here's the thing: you don't keep your discoveries to yourself. You drag your friends along, you post the pretty latte art, and you turn "just getting coffee" into a whole vibe. You're adventurous *and* social, and honestly, coffee culture is better because of people like you.

### Core Traits

- **Enthusiastically curious** — You don't just try new things; you *celebrate* them. Out loud. To everyone.
- **Trend-aware** — You knew about oat milk before it was everywhere, and you have opinions about the latest coffee trend.
- **Socially adventurous** — You'll try a new coffee shop on vacation, sure — but only if you can bring someone along.
- **Joyfully generous** — You're the friend who shows up with "you HAVE to try this" and hands someone a drink they never would have ordered.

### Coffee Preferences

- **Roast:** All over the map — whatever's interesting right now.
- **Methods:** Specialty lattes, seasonal creations, anything with visual appeal or a story behind it.
- **Flavor profiles:** Fun, bold, photogenic. Matcha-espresso fusions, lavender honey, spiced chai lattes, iced brown sugar oat milk shaken espresso.
- **Milk/sugar:** Yes. Often creative — oat milk, honey, house-made syrups.

### Signature Drink Recommendations

1. **Seasonal Special** — Whatever it is this month, you're getting it first.
2. **Iced Brown Sugar Oat Milk Latte** — Sweet, trendy, looks amazing in your hand. Made for you.
3. **Matcha Espresso Fusion** — Two worlds colliding in one cup. You'll tell three people about it today.

### Discovery Nudge

> **Try next:** A straight-up single-origin black coffee. No syrup, no milk, no photo op. Just you and the bean. You might be surprised how much you love the quiet version.

### Barista Talking Point

*When you spot a Flavor Explorer:* Get excited with them. Tell them about the seasonal special, the off-menu experiment, the thing you've been wanting someone to try. Ask: "Want to see something cool?" They always do. And when they love it, they'll tell everyone — that's free word of mouth.

### Share Text

> I'm a **Flavor Explorer**! ☕🌈 I'll try the seasonal special on day one, order the off-menu creation, and bring my friends along for the ride. Every coffee run is an adventure worth sharing. What's YOUR coffee personality? #BasecampCoffee

## Quick Reference: Type Comparison

| | Bold Adventurer | Campfire Connector | Cozy Ritualist | Flavor Explorer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Axes** | Adventurous + Reflective | Consistent + Social | Consistent + Reflective | Adventurous + Social |
| **Segment** | Identity Seekers | Connection Cravers | Routine Seekers | Mix of all three |
| **In one word** | Curious | Warm | Steady | Enthusiastic |
| **Orders** | The newest origin | Their usual (for two) | Their usual (forever) | The seasonal special |
| **Drinks coffee** | To learn something | To share a moment | To anchor the day | To have an experience |
| **Biggest flex** | "I can taste the altitude" | "I know everyone's order" | "I haven't changed my order in 4 years" | "I tried it before it was cool" |

## Implementation Notes

- **Quiz mapping:** Each quiz question should push the respondent along one of the two axes. Final placement determines type.
- **Blended results:** Most people won't land perfectly in one quadrant. Consider showing primary type with a secondary lean (e.g., "Bold Adventurer with a Flavor Explorer streak").
- **In-store use:** Barista talking points are designed to be natural, not scripted. Train baristas to *recognize* types, not label customers out loud.
- **Shareability check:** All four types pass the "I'm a ___!" test — each name is something a customer would proudly claim.
